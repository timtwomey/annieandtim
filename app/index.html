Why France? we hear you ask.

Well, several years ago we took a motorbike trip through the Dordogne region and simply put, we fell in love with the natural beauty of the area. The wine, the cheese, the food, the castles on every hilltop; this just sealed the deal. After 3 weeks of over indulgence, budget hostels and chaffing leather pants, we vowed to come back someday to do it with a little more style. So what more suitable a time than for our wedding!

As the old adage goes, time flies when you're having fun - this is definitely the case with us. Nine years, several jobs, houses, a world tour, some questionable fashion choices, and a litany of rescue cats later, we are overjoyed to enter this next chapter of our lives together. We can't think of a better way to kick things off than a weekend in France with our nearest and dearest; you are after all, the people who made us who we are. Truly, nothing could make us happier than to have you all there to celebrate with us.

Location

Chateau de Cautine, near Saint-Privat, Correze is situated in a rural location. The bigger nearby towns are Argentat, Tulle and Brive-la-Gaillarde. It is advisable to have a car to get around.

Travel

Flying

There are a number of flight options depending on where you're coming from. From Dublin, Aer Lingus flies to Bordeaux and Toulouse. Ryanair flies to Tours and Rodez, which is closer. There is also the option to fly to Paris and catch the TGV to Brive. From London there are additional options with Ryanair, flying to Brive la Gaillarde, Limoges and Bergerac. Note that not all flights run on daily schedules. Also, as we're planning on a mid-afternoon celebration, there is the option of getting flights on the morning of the 13th.

Sailing & Driving

If you'd rather drive over, Irish Ferries and Brittany Ferries operate routes from Ireland to either Roscoff or Cherbourg. From the UK there are more options again, including the Eurotunnel.

Getting Around

Once on French soil, the most preferable mode of transport would be to hire a car. From the main airports listed, expect the drive to be between an hour or three depending on distance.

  • Argus Car Hire
Bus & Train

Taking the train or bus is also an option from the main cities. By train, to Brive la Gaillarde or Aurillac from London via Paris the journey takes 8 hours, then bus to the village. There are 2 buses a day from outside Brive station to St Privat: 14.30 and 18.20pm. There are no buses on Sunday or public holidays. Bus fare: €2 and we can pick you up from the village.

  • SNCF Trains
  • Bus Timetable

If you are in need of transportation in France, please give us your flight details and we can assess if there is demand for organising a private bus. Car rental is recommended however, as the area is quite remote and there are many sights you may want to visit which can only be reached by car.
